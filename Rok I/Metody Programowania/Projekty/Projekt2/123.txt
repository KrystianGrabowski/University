 Znalezione w internecie, to chyba o to chodzi:
Just use write_canonical/1 and you see why:

?- write_canonical((1, 2, 3, 4)).
','(1, ','(2, ','(3, 4)))
Yes (0.00s cpu)

?- write_canonical(((1, 2), 3, 4)).
','(','(1, 2), ','(3, 4))
Yes (0.00s cpu)

Your 'tuples' are trees made out of ','/2 terms. If you want
proper flat tuples, use functors with the corresponding arity.
